The Source Of My Creativity

Creativity can come from a variety of sources, including: Individual factors These include intellectual abilities, knowledge, personality, motivation, and styles of thinking. Environmental factors These include education, training, role models, mentors, and even childhood trauma. Creative techniques These include brainstorming, lateral thinking, and brainwriting: Brainstorming: A common technique that involves suspending judgment and writing down ideas and solutions. Lateral thinking: Involves looking at a situation in a different way. Brainwriting: Similar to brainstorming, but involves a group identifying a problem and then writing down ideas.

Thinking Out Of The Box

Thinking outside the box" is an idiom that means to think creatively and unconventionally, often by looking at a problem from a new perspective. It can also refer to thinking freely and off the beaten path.

Determining your objective/goal

When determining a creativity goal, you can consider the following: S.M.A.R.T. method: Set goals that are specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and timely. Process goals: Consider what small steps you can take regularly to help you reach your goal. Schedule these steps consistently and track your progress with a habit tracker. Visualization: Spend time visualizing what you want to achieve. Celebrate wins: Celebrate your wins, both big and small.
